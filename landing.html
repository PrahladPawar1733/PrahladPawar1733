Why do we need Sleep

Sleep is an essential function that allows your body and mind to recharge, leaving you refreshed and alert when you wake up. Healthy sleep also helps the body remain healthy and stave off diseases. Without enough sleep, the brain cannot function properly. Most adults require between seven and nine hours3 of nightly sleep. Children and teenagers need substantially more sleep, mainly if they are younger than five years of age. Work schedules, day-to-day stressors, a disruptive bedroom environment, and medical conditions can all prevent us from receiving enough sleep. For most adults, at least seven hours of sleep7 each night is needed for proper cognitive and behavioural functions. An insufficient amount of sleep can lead to severe repercussions. Some studies have shown sleep deprivation leaves people vulnerable to attention lapses, reduced cognition, delayed reactions, and mood shifts.

Music and Sleep

Sleep is vital for human health and wellbeing, and sleep disturbances are comorbid to many mental and physiological disorders. Music consistently improves subjective sleep quality, whereas results for objective sleep parameters diverge. Listening to music is a widely used tool to improve sleep. In an online survey in a general population, 62% stated to have at least once used music to help them sleep.
Sleepify’s suggestion of sound to sleep by:
1)Ocean sounds: For many people, the rhythmic crashing of water onto sand and rock can be meditative—and meditation carries some surprising health benefits.
2) Nature Sounds: Scientists at Britain’s University of Sussex had participants listen to recordings of nature sounds and artificial sounds while measuring their brain and nervous system activity. They found nature sounds led to more outward-focused attention in the brain rather than inward-focused.
